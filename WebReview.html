WEB REVIEW
Review of FINN
1. Introduction
For my website review I chose a company where I worked before called FINN. FINN is a start-up from Germany that was established in 2019. Their business model is to provide a subscription model for cars.

What does that mean?
In particular, you can order a car on the FINN website and it will be delivered to your doorstep. Insurance, maintenance, taxes, roadside assistance and much more are covered by the monthly subscription. The only thing left to pay for is the fuel or electricity for e-cars.
2. Interactive Website Design
FINN's website design is not only held minimalistic but also is also very functional. It shows important information such as voice of customers, popular brands, advantages using FINN and FAQs.
3. Product List Page
Looking at the product list page (PLP) one can see there are many UX/UI design best practices used.
  1. Navigation bar at the top is always visible gives high value to the user to navigate quickly and efficiently through the website
  2. Opportunities to see if one is logged in is useful to quickly access ones profile
  3. Useful and simple filter options to find relevant products quickly throughout the page is a must have
  4. Product cards only show most relevant information making skimming through the product quickly possible
  5. A sorting option helps to prioritize products
The PLP is designed to maximize efficiency, but also be minimalist and not overload information.
4. Product Details Page
The product Detail Page (PDP) is also very well structured. It becomes apparent that the style is similar when looking into the clean design of e.g. apple.com.

Prominent features are:

  • Breadcrumbs at the top
  • Clear pictures of the product
  • Visbility of all options while maintaining clarity
  • Prominent Call To Action
  • Advantages of the product as bullets
5. Conclusion and recommendation
FINN achieved an intuitive, clean and usable website by following many design best practices. While going through the website, I didn't feel lost in any moment and felt that lots of thoughts are made where to put which information. My only recommendation would be to bring the website to the next level by introducing for example a Chat-Bot or a live Chat support.
